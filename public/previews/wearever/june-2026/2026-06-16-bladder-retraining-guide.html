5 STEPS TO RETRAIN YOUR BLADDER

1

Pause before you go

When the urge hits, wait a moment instead of going right away. This gently teaches your brain that every urge is not an emergency. Start slow and never push to discomfort.
2

Keep a simple diary

Jot down when you drink and when you visit the bathroom. A notebook or a note on your phone works fine. After a week you will see your real patterns clearly.
3

Set small goals

Start where you are. If you go every 30 minutes, try stretching to 45 for a few weeks before reaching further. Small, steady wins add up faster than big leaps.
4

Be patient with yourself

This is a marathon, not a sprint. Progress can take weeks or months, and a setback is just part of the process. Focus on the wins and keep going.
5

Add Kegel exercises

Strengthening the pelvic floor supports the whole effort. Kegels are discreet and easy. Do them while sitting, watching TV, or driving. The more you do, the stronger you get.

Guidance drawn from the Bladder and Bowel Foundation and the Interstitial Cystitis Association. Always check with your own doctor about what is right for you.
