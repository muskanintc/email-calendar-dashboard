Work Smarter

Splitting Wood Shouldn't Wreck Your Back

If you burn firewood, you know the feeling. One afternoon at the splitter and you pay for it all week. Here is what most people get wrong, and the two tool setup that fixes it.

The Insight

It Is Not the Splitting. It Is the Bending.

On any given splitting day, most of the wear on your back does not come from the swing. It comes from the moves between swings.

Bend to pick up the round. Bend to load it on the splitter. Bend to grab the splits off the ground. Multiply that by a cord of wood and you are talking hundreds of reps at the worst possible angle for your lower back.

The smart move is not to swing harder. It is to take the bending out of the equation.

The Setup

Two Tools. Zero Bending.

1

Lift the round with the Dan-D Lift.

Bring heavy rounds up to splitter height without hoisting them yourself.
2

Split at waist height on the TimberOX.

Work upright. No crouching, no dragging, no dropping rounds on your boots.
