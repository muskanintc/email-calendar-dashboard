Stack It Now or Burn
Wet Wood in December.

Firewood needs 6 to 12 months to dry. That is not a suggestion. That is the difference between a clean, hot burn and a smoking, creosote-filled chimney.

Wood stacked in May is seasoned by November. Wood stacked in September is not. The clock started this month. Every week you wait is a week your wood does not dry.

Stacking 101

Three Rules. Dry Wood Every Time.

1

Off the Ground

Wood on the ground absorbs moisture from the soil. Elevation lets air circulate underneath.

2

Airflow on All Sides

No tarps wrapped tight. No stacking against walls. Wind and sun do the drying. Let them in.

3

Bark Side Up

Bark sheds rain naturally. Top row bark-up, with a cover, keeps water out and moisture moving.

You did the hard work. You felled it, bucked it, hauled it, split it. Do not waste all of that by stacking it wrong. Wood piled on the ground, wrapped in a tarp, or leaning against the garage does not season. It rots. It grows mold. And when you try to burn it, it smokes, burns cold, and builds creosote in your chimney.

Creosote buildup is not just inefficient. It is a chimney fire waiting to happen. Proper stacking is the simplest thing you can do to protect your work and your home.
