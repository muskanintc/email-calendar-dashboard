It Comes Down To Four Things

Routing. Durability. Audio Delivery. Compatibility.

Here is what that actually looks like, side by side.

Option A

Acoustic Tube

Option B

In-Ear (HDLO / AWARE)

01  Routing

Audio travels from an external speaker up a coiled tube into the ear. The tube runs visibly along the collar or plate carrier.

HDLO uses a direct driver in the ear. AWARE runs a thin wire that carries audio straight to the ear. No tube, no external speaker housing.

02  Durability

The tube is a wear point. It can crack, kink, or collect moisture and debris over time. Replacement tubes are standard.

No tube means one less failure point. The in-ear body and wire are built to ride through a full shift without the tube pinch issues.

03  Audio Delivery

Sound travels through a length of tube before it reaches the ear. Proven, familiar, works in the vast majority of standard radio setups.

Audio is delivered direct. Tighter, clearer transmissions, especially useful in loud environments where tube setups can sound thin.

04  Compatibility

Pairs with most standard PTT microphones and two way radio systems. The go to for teams that need a shared, familiar setup across the unit.

HDLO and AWARE are built to run with Sheepdog PTT systems. Designed end to end so the earpiece and the mic speak the same language.
